SafeCell – Privacy Policy & Terms of Service

Privacy Policy

Data Controller

The Data Controller is SafeCell, contactable at: safecellitaliainfo@gmail.com

Data Collected

  • Identification data: first name, last name, tax code, identity document;
  • Contact data: email, phone number;
  • Device data: device type, device number, IMEI;
  • Authentication data: encrypted password;
  • Location data: real-time GPS coordinates.

Purposes of Processing

Data is used for:

  • user registration and authentication;
  • device tracking service;
  • support for Law Enforcement after filing an official report;
  • management of security communications and notifications;
  • account protection through biometric authentication.

Legal Basis

User consent and legitimate interest for the protection of devices.

Data Retention

Data is permanently deleted in case of cancellation (disdetta).

Data Sharing

Data is not sold. It may only be shared with:

  • Law Enforcement, after verifying the filed police report;
  • technology providers (such as Firebase) bound by confidentiality obligations.

Security

We use encryption, controlled access, and high security standards to protect your data.

User Rights (GDPR)

The user has the right to:

  • access;
  • rectification;
  • erasure;
  • data portability;
  • objection.

To exercise these rights, you can contact: safecellitaliainfo@gmail.com

International Transfers

Use of Google/Firebase servers outside the EU may occur under Standard Contractual Clauses.

Terms of Service

Service Use

The app helps the user track their own device in case of loss or theft. Active tracking for Law Enforcement takes place only after verification of the police report. The monitoring link is valid for 96 hours, and can be extended.

Limitation of Liability

SafeCell does not guarantee the certain recovery of lost or stolen devices.

Cancellation

The user may revoke the service and delete all data through the “Disdetta” function by entering their unique code.

Changes

SafeCell may modify this notice, providing prior communication where appropriate.

Governing Law

Applicable law: GDPR and Italian law – competent court: Rome (Italy).
